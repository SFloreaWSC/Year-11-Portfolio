Development Log



Task 1 - Store

Development

When I first started to develop the store portion of the encounter loop, I added the three possible purchasable options, being an elixir, sharpening of the player’s sword, and the patching of their garments. These all upgraded the three stats of the player for a cost, coming in the terms of coins. These stats were the player’s health, attack, and defense. Coins are gained by defeating enemies in combat, which are the sections of the game that follow the safe zone sections, the harder enemies giving more coins than the easier ones, which will be touched on further in task 3. The player can choose to buy something or leave the store, which progresses to the end of the safe zone section. After adding the items I added the ability to actually purchase these items, changing your stats accordingly. Once this was finished, the prices of items were turned into variables rather than just random numbers so they could be used for both the price check and the listen price the merchant says. Finally, I actually made it so you needed the correct amount of coins to purchase the respective items.

Task 2 - Random Item

Development

One of the two safe zone sections that the player can encounter is an area with a random item which will boost either their attack or defense. To not make the store obsolete, there is not an item to boost one’s health, and when one stat is raised, the other is lowered to give a trade-off effect similar to the store. I started coding this section by adding the three different sections for the different possible write ups, as the randomized name of the safe zone locations could require different contexts depending on the implied location. I followed this by creating the different choices for either picking up the item or not and the following reward/consequence. Unfortunately, this is where I ran into a massive issue, as seen in the following section, that I could not fix for days until I did… somehow. Also unfortunately, because I could not figure out the issue for so long I had become very upset and forgot to take screenshots of my many attempts to fix it, but even if I had, I’d have to omit most of them due to the sheer number of changes I made.

Task 3 - Combat

Development

To develop the combat function for the enemy sections of the game, I started by adding three enemy types of varying difficulty. The enemies in order of difficulty go as following; Bat, Chamois, and Gray Wolf. The three different types of enemies were added to have more and less difficulty types of enemies, as well as having more randomization with a wider portfolio of enemies one could encounter. All of the species chosen for the enemy choices are found in European forests, mainly just for realism and an easter egg for any keen players. Originally, I was going to have each enemy be its own code block, but then I realized that was ridiculous and consolidated it into one block with variable names instead. After this, I added code to give the properties of the different enemies, like attack damage and health, before the rest of the code so that they would each be different.

Improvments

The fighting sections now work every time and both the player and enemy do and take damage. The wolf’s health was a syntax error in the randomization of the health value and enemies now ‘miss’ instead of doing negative damage.

The enemies now have a higher chance of dealing more damage and defense does less to help the player, creating a more difficult combat section as it was far too easy before.

Certain enemies will now only appear before and after a specific amount of level has been passed, creating more progression in the game and requiring the player to use upgrades wisely. The coins for each enemy were also altered to reflect the feedback. I have also added the lynx, a fourth enemy.
